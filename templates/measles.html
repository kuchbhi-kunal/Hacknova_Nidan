    Solution

    Esca is a complex disease whose symptoms arise from structural and physiological changes that cannot be reduced to a simple scheme of cause and effect. Consequently, symptoms that look alike may have different causes. Apart from the occasional delayed sprouting of esca-affected vines in the spring, external symptoms in the Northern Hemisphere develop on the crown between June and September, either through the entire vine or on single branches.
